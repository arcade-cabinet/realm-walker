## Overview

The world's history is organized into **7 narrative ages** spanning from primordial creation to the modern apocalypse. Each age has distinct visual themes, dominant factions, and story focus. Players explore these ages through **B Story time travel**, unlocked by collecting Guardian boons in the **A Story**.

**Key Principle**: Ages are **thematic and narrative-driven**, not bound to specific years. What matters is the emotional journey and visual contrast between ages.

## The 7 Ages (Reverse Chronological)

### Age 1: Age of Sundering (Year 2100+, 0 Boons)

**Theme**: Dead world, frozen apocalypse, last hope  
**Visual**: Ice, corpses, ruins, eternal darkness, 90% extinction  
**Story Focus**: A Story (Guardian collection), meeting survivors

**Factions Present:**

- **Order**: Sanctum remnants (~200 people), scattered Crimson thralls
- **Cult**: All 6 cults succeeded - Destroyer awakening
- **Neutral**: Compact animals (Ottermere, badgers, foxes)

**Narrative Role**: Starting point, horror atmosphere, sets stakes

---

### Age 2: Age of Invention (2 Boons Required)

**Theme**: Human-gnome alliance, last of magic fading, industrial-sacred fusion  
**Visual**: Workshops, crystal projectors, sacred geometry, gnomish mechanisms, twilight warmth  
**Continents**: Both accessible

**Order Faction:**

- **Sanctum of Sacred Invention** (Gearhart)
  - Formation: Humans of Dawnshield ally with last gnomes
  - Goal: Preserve gnomish knowledge as gnomes fade from world
  - Technology: Sacred geometry + gnomish engineering
  - Population: ~50,000 (mixed human/gnome)
  - Conflict: Crimson Pact raids for thralls
  - Connected Guardian: Divine Smith (Cogsworth)

**Cult Faction:**

- **Crimson Pact** (Carmilla, 847 years old)
  - Status: Peak power, 40,000 thralls
  - Goal: Eternal night through shard power
  - Philosophy: Blood aristocracy, consent through thralldom
  - Conflict: Needs constant thralls, raids Sanctum territories
  - Connected Guardian: None directly (predates Guardian system)

**Story Themes:**

- Last collaboration between humans and magical creatures
- Technology preserving dying magic
- Vampire ethics (happy thralls vs consent)
- Gnomes fading mysteriously (echoes of Age of Kings crusades)

**Mythology Echoes Found Here:**

- Strange tensions between gnomes and humans (unexplained)
- References to "the crusades" (cryptic)
- Gnomish reluctance to discuss the past

---

### Age 3: Age of Kings - Late/Middle (3 Boons Required)

**Theme**: Holy crusades tail end, zealous humanity, moral complexity  
**Visual**: Gothic castles, burning pyres, battlefields, zealot armies  
**Continents**: Lumina (human dominance), Umbra (cult refuges forming)

**Order Faction:**

- **Dawnshield Order** (Young Aurelius, not yet cursed)
  - Formation: Paladins united under "purification" doctrine
  - Goal: Eliminate all "unnatural" beings (very broad definition)
  - Philosophy: Light through fire, purity through blood
  - Conflict: Hunting remaining magical creatures
  - Connected Guardian: Sun Warden (binds Aurelius later)

**Cult Factions:**

- **Crimson Pact Formation** (Carmilla's fall)

  - Origin: Dawnshield paladin (Carmilla) falls to pride
  - Transformation: Drinks shard-tainted blood, becomes vampire
  - Philosophy: Rejects zealotry, embraces "honest" predation
  - Theme: Order's first major corruption

- **Ossuary Praxis** (Morteus, royal physician)
  - Status: Active, conducting "resurrection research"
  - Goal: Defeat death through shard-preserved tissue
  - Philosophy: Identity is negotiable, flesh is eternal
  - Conflict: Medical experiments horrify even Dawnshield

**Story Themes:**

- Witness Crimson Pact formation (tragic fall)
- Understand Dawnshield's dark origins (not always heroes)
- See moral complexity (crusades had "reasons")
- Meet Ossuary at peak ethical horror

**Player Experience:**

- Defend human villages from magical creature raids
- Realize humans are also perpetrators
- Witness Carmilla's transformation
- Question simple good/evil

---

### Age 4: Age of Kings - Early (4 Boons Required)

**Theme**: Human ascendance, first crusades, magical creatures vs humanity  
**Visual**: Early castles, tribal warfare, forests burning, species extinction beginning  
**Continents**: Contested territories

**Order Faction:**

- **Proto-Dawnshield** (First Kings of Dawn)
  - Formation: Human tribes unite under "divine mandate"
  - Goal: Claim world from "monsters"
  - Philosophy: Humans are Creator's chosen
  - Conflict: Everyone non-human

**Cult Faction:**

- **Ossuary Praxis Formation**
  - Origin: Royal physician exiled for experiments
  - First Act: Successful resurrection surgery
  - Philosophy: Death is negotiable
  - Theme: Science divorced from ethics

**Story Themes:**

- First organized human kingdoms
- Magical creatures fighting for survival
- Neither side purely right/wrong
- Seeds of future conflicts planted

**Player Experience:**

- Defend villages from raids (both sides raid)
- See formation of Ossuary (medical horror)
  - Understand inevitability of conflict
- Witness first extinctions

---

### Age 5: Age of Stone - Late (5 Boons Required)

**Theme**: Dwarven supremacy, early humanity, last elves fading  
**Visual**: Mountain kingdoms, deep forges, isolated human settlements, lone elf singing  
**Continents**: Umbra (dwarven), Lumina (contested)

**Order Faction:**

- **Ironbound Covenant** (General Thrain, 267 years old)
  - Status: Dwarven kingdoms at peak
  - Goal: Mine world, build eternal strongholds
  - Philosophy: Stone endures, family is everything
  - Conflict: Peaceful but territorial
  - Connected Guardian: Stone Warden

**Cult Faction:**

- **Ashen Choir** (Maestro Mortis, last elf)
  - Status: SOLO, singing endless lament
  - Origin: Last elf who chose to stay when others left
  - Philosophy: Beauty in sorrow, art transcends
  - Conflict: None (just exists, singing)
  - Connected Guardian: Eternal Note

**Story Themes:**

- Dwarven civilization at height
- Massive mining reshaping continents
- Last elves leaving for higher realms
- One who stayed, regretting choice
- Humans just beginning to emerge

**Player Experience:**

- Witness dwarven grandeur
- Meet isolated human tribes
- Hear Maestro's eternal song (haunting)
- Understand elven exodus

---

### Age 6: Age of Stone - Early (6 Boons Required)

**Theme**: Elven-dwarven alliance, forests dying, cults emerging  
**Visual**: Last great forests burning, elven cities in trees, early dwarven mines, fire cults  
**Continents**: Lumina (elven/dwarven), Umbra (forests dying)

**Order Factions:**

- **Radiant Collegium** (Luminara, 312 years old)

  - Status: Elven magical academy at peak
  - Goal: Preserve knowledge as forests die
  - Philosophy: Mathematics = truth
  - Conflict: Cannot stop forest destruction
  - Connected Guardian: Twin Gods

- **Early Ironbound** (Proto-dwarves)
  - Status: Emerging from stone, mining begins
  - Goal: Claim mountains, build underground
  - Conflict: Mining kills forests unintentionally

**Cult Faction:**

- **Eclipsed Flame** (Pyrophant Ignatius)
  - Status: Active fire cult, consuming forests
  - Origin: Formed from corrupted druids
  - Philosophy: Destroyer as purifying fire
  - Conflict: Burning continent-scale forests
  - Connected Guardian: None (pre-Guardian awareness)

**Story Themes:**

- Alliance between elves and dwarves
- Both inadvertently destroying world (magic vs mining)
- Pyrophant consuming Umbra forests
- Elves preparing to leave
- Last of natural world dying

**Player Experience:**

- Fight Pyrophant's fire cultists
- Witness last of Umbra's forests burn
- See elven-dwarven cooperation
- Understand environmental catastrophe
- One continent falls to cults permanently

---

### Age 7: Age of Song (7 Boons Required)

**Theme**: Continent-spanning forests, World Tree connecting continents, elves at peak, corruption begins  
**Visual**: Massive forests, World Tree visible from everywhere, vibrant life, elven cities in harmony  
**Continents**: Connected by World Tree branches

**Order Factions:**

- **Circle of Verdant Spirits** (Thalindra Rootsong, young)

  - Status: Druids protecting World Tree
  - Goal: Maintain balance, protect all life
  - Philosophy: All life is sacred
  - Conflict: Corruption spreading in ranks
  - Connected Guardian: Forest Ancient (Thalindra merges later)

- **Elven Dominions** (Multiple kingdoms)
  - Status: Peak civilization, singing magic
  - Goal: Create beauty, preserve harmony
  - Philosophy: Song shapes reality
  - Conflict: Growing darkness on Umbra

**Cult Factions:**

- **Veilbound Synod Formation** (Seraph, corrupted druid)

  - Origin: Druid Seraph corrupted by Destroyer whispers during tree communion
  - Philosophy: Knowledge at any cost, prophecy obsession
  - Role: Wisdom to Pyrophant's might
  - Theme: Fear of unknown leads to corruption
  - Connected Guardian: Mist Walker

- **Eclipsed Flame Formation** (First Pyrophant)
  - Origin: Seraph's followers create first sacrificial pyres
  - First Pyrophant: Druid who immolated self in holy fire
  - Philosophy: Rebirth through fire, Destroyer as natural force
  - Theme: Ecstatic masochism, zealotry

**Story Themes:**

- World at peak beauty before corruption
- Witness Seraph's fall (fear-driven)
- See Pyrophant's formation (sacrifice)
- Understand how darkness spreads
- Last time both continents were green

**Player Experience:**

- See World Tree connecting continents
- Witness Seraph's corruption
- Watch first Pyrophant emerge from flames
- Failed attempt to save Umbra
- Forced retreat to Lumina

---

### Age 8: Age of Gods (8 Boons Required - Primordial)

**Theme**: Creator vs Destroyer directly, world forming, first war  
**Visual**: Toxic hellscape, one continent, World Tree erupting, primordial chaos  
**Continents**: Single supercontinent, hot and expanding

**Order Factions:**

- **Original Circle of Verdant Spirits** (Thalindra forming)

  - Origin: Created by Creator from World Tree bark
  - Goal: Shape world away from Destroyer's image
  - Philosophy: Life over death, order over chaos
  - Theme: First beings of light
  - Connected Guardian: Forest Ancient (future merge)

- **Veilwalkers** (Umbra Nightwhisper, reformed)
  - Origin: Demonic race born from Destroyer, first to defect
  - Goal: Protect druids, atone for origin
  - Philosophy: Redemption through service
  - Theme: First betrayal of Destroyer
  - Conflict: Zealously hunt former kin
  - Connected Guardian: Mist Walker (watches betrayal)

**Cult Faction:**

- **Children of Hollow** (First beings)
  - Origin: Born from Destroyer's toxic thoughts
  - Nature: Demonic, ethereal, least shaped by world
  - Goal: Destroy Creation, return to void
  - Philosophy: Existence is suffering
  - Theme: First cult, purest evil
  - Connected Guardian: Silent Keeper

**Story Themes:**

- Creator's direct intervention (only time)
- World Tree shatters supercontinent (Lumina/Umbra formed)
- First war (demons vs druids)
- First betrayal (Veilwalkers defect)
- Origins of all conflict
- Why world is broken from start

**Player Experience:**

- Witness Creator commanding World Tree
- See supercontinent shatter
- Meet primordial demons
- Understand Veilwalkers' shame/zealotry
- Realize world was NEVER whole
- Complete mythology understanding

- Age of Gods: Formation (first druids)
- Connected: Thalindra across all ages

**Ossuary Praxis:**

- Age of Kings Late: Active (medical horror)
- Age of Kings Early: Formation (exiled physician)
- Earlier: Doesn't exist

**Ashen Choir:**

- Age of Stone Late: Maestro solo (last elf)
- Age of Stone Early: Forming (elves leaving)

**Eclipsed Flame:**

- Age of Stone Early: Active (burning forests)

- Age of Song: Formation (Seraph corrupted)

**Children of Hollow:**

## C Story Integration (Raven Corsairs)

### Raven Presence Across Ages

**Key Principle**: Ravens broke the Compact and exist outside normal time. They appear as pirates/outcasts in EVERY age, but with different aesthetics matching that age.

**Age-Specific Raven Variants:**

- **Age of Sundering**: Diesel pirates, frozen ships
- **Age of Invention**: Steam pirates, mechanical raiders
- **Age of Kings**: Medieval corsairs, organized navies
- **Age of Stone**: Dwarven smugglers, mountain bandits
- **Age of Song**: Elven pirates, tree-ship raiders
- **Age of Gods**: Primordial outcasts, chaos agents

**C Story Triggers:**

- 1+ B stories complete: First Raven encounter
- 4+ B stories complete: Raven betrayal story revealed
- 8+ B stories complete: Raven redemption offer
- 12 B stories complete: Final Raven ally for apocalypse

**Narrative Role**: C Story weaves through B Story exploration, providing moral counterpoint (those who rejected destiny vs those who accepted burden).

## Mythology Echo System

### How Echoes Work

**In A Story** (dead world, Year 2100+):

- Player finds props/ruins with historical significance
- Clicking triggers "mythology echo" - brief vision/text
- Echo provides: Age name, faction hint, visual clue

**Example Echo Progression:**

**After Guardian 1** (Stone Warden, 2 boons):

```
[Player clicks ornate goblet in ruins]
Badger Elder: "Beautiful no? Such craftsmanship came not
from the hands of your kind. You don't get such delicate
work from the hands of a man."

Player: "Who then? You?"

Badger: "Bah. I am but a digger. But if you think yours
are the first feet to walk this world, you are more fool
than I thought."

[Echo stored: "Age of Invention - Gnomish craftsmanship"]
[B Story unlocked: Can visit Age of Invention]
```

**Echo Types by Age:**

- **Age of Invention**: Gnomish tools, crystal projectors, sacred geometry
- **Age of Kings**: Crusader banners, execution blocks, zealot texts
- **Age of Stone**: Dwarven runes, mountain carvings, elven songs (faint)
- **Age of Song**: Forest artifacts, World Tree branches (fossilized)
- **Age of Gods**: Primordial corruption, demonic artifacts, Creator's marks
