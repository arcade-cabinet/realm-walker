# The Twelve Lands - Geography of the Sundered World

**Version**: 1.0  
**Status**: FOUNDATIONAL LORE  
**Created**: October 6, 2025  
**Purpose**: Define the territorial geography and survival strategies of each faction

---

## The Tortured World

**80 years without the sun.**

No natural light. No natural heat. Life clings to existence through:
- **Magic** (benevolent or selfish)
- **Ingenuity** (dwarven forges, gnomish technology)
- **Geothermal** (volcanic regions, lava flows)
- **Planar rifts** (otherworldly energy)
- **Underground** (deep earth warmth)

**Areas without these sources = frozen death.**

## The Twelve Lands

### 🌅 LIGHT FACTION TERRITORIES

#### 1. The Radiant Bastions (Dawnshield Order)
**Location**: Central highlands, elevated plateaus  
**Light Source**: Holy magic - artificial sun crystals  
**Heat Source**: Blessed flames, never-dying braziers  
**Geography**: Fortified mountain keeps, terraced farms under crystal domes  
**Population**: 50,000 humans, mostly refugees they've protected  
**Climate**: Temperate (magically maintained), eternal golden twilight  

**Survival Strategy**: Massive holy crystals project artificial sunlight across their territory. Blessed flames provide heat. They protect the largest refugee population, feeding them through magically-enhanced agriculture under crystal domes.

#### 2. The Verdant Sanctum (Circle of Verdant Spirits)
**Location**: Ancient forests, still alive through druidic magic  
**Light Source**: Bioluminescent flora, World Tree glow  
**Heat Source**: Geothermal hot springs, living warmth  
**Geography**: Dense forests with glowing vegetation, tree cities  
**Population**: 30,000 druids, fey, forest dwellers  
**Climate**: Humid, warm (geothermally heated), perpetual green twilight  

**Survival Strategy**: The World Tree's roots tap deep geothermal energy. Druids channel this into bioluminescent plants that provide light. The forest itself generates warmth through magical photosynthesis in reverse.

#### 3. The Iron Holds (Ironbound Covenant)
**Location**: Deep mountain fortresses, underground cities  
**Light Source**: Forge fires, industrial lighting  
**Heat Source**: Volcanic forges, magma channels  
**Geography**: Massive underground complexes, surface fortifications  
**Population**: 80,000 dwarves, the largest single faction  
**Climate**: Hot (forge-heated), smoky, industrial  

**Survival Strategy**: Dwarven engineering at its finest. They've tapped into volcanic magma flows, using them to power massive forges that provide both light and heat. Underground cities are warm and bright through pure ingenuity, no magic required.

#### 4. The Luminous Spires (Radiant Collegium)
**Location**: Floating crystal cities, tethered to mountaintops  
**Light Source**: Arcane light magic, perpetual spell-light  
**Heat Source**: Magical heating, thermal wards  
**Geography**: Floating platforms connected by light bridges  
**Population**: 20,000 mages, scholars, apprentices  
**Climate**: Controlled (magically perfect), bright as day  

**Survival Strategy**: Pure arcane power. Mages maintain constant spells that generate light and heat. Their cities float above the frozen ground, tethered to peaks. Everything is magical - light, heat, even the air they breathe is spell-sustained.

#### 5. The Twilight Paths (Veilwalkers)
**Location**: Shadow-lands between territories, the in-between  
**Light Source**: Minimal - they prefer darkness  
**Heat Source**: Stolen warmth, magical cloaks  
**Geography**: Fog-shrouded valleys, hidden paths  
**Population**: 5,000 assassins, spies, wanderers  
**Climate**: Cold, dark, misty  

**Survival Strategy**: They don't fight the cold - they embrace it. Magical cloaks provide personal warmth. They survive by trading information and services to other factions, taking payment in food, heat crystals, and shelter access.

#### 6. The Crystal Foundries (Sanctum of Sacred Invention)
**Location**: Coastal cliffs, sea-level workshops  
**Light Source**: Magitech fusion reactors  
**Heat Source**: Crystal power cores  
**Geography**: Massive workshops, construct factories  
**Population**: 15,000 gnomes, inventors, constructs  
**Climate**: Warm (reactor-heated), bright (crystal-lit)  

**Survival Strategy**: They've cracked magitech fusion - combining magic and technology to create self-sustaining power cores. These provide endless light and heat. Their constructs don't need either, making them perfect laborers.

### 🌑 DARK FACTION TERRITORIES

#### 7. The Profane Depths (Veilbound Synod)
**Location**: Underground caverns, eldritch temples  
**Light Source**: Bioluminescent fungi, eldritch glow  
**Heat Source**: Summoned heat from outer realms  
**Geography**: Twisted caverns, non-Euclidean architecture  
**Population**: 10,000 cultists, corrupted scholars  
**Climate**: Warm (otherworldly), dim purple-green light  

**Survival Strategy**: They've opened rifts to outer realms, siphoning warmth and strange light from beyond reality. Their fungi feed on eldritch energy. Madness is the price, but they survive.

#### 8. The Crimson Courts (Crimson Pact)
**Location**: Gothic cities, vampire strongholds  
**Light Source**: Blood-fueled lanterns, crimson glow  
**Heat Source**: Stolen life energy  
**Geography**: Victorian architecture, underground blood vaults  
**Population**: 8,000 vampires, 40,000 thralls  
**Climate**: Cool (vampires prefer it), dim red light  

**Survival Strategy**: Vampires don't need heat - they're undead. Their thralls survive through blood magic that converts life force into warmth. Lanterns burn blood for light. It's horrific but effective.

#### 9. The Resonance Halls (Ashen Choir)
**Location**: Acoustic caverns, sound-sculpted cities  
**Light Source**: Sonic luminescence, vibration glow  
**Heat Source**: Resonance heating, sound-to-thermal conversion  
**Geography**: Cathedral-like caverns, perfect acoustics  
**Population**: 12,000 sonic mages, death choir members  
**Climate**: Warm (vibration-heated), glowing with sound  

**Survival Strategy**: They've discovered that certain frequencies generate heat and light. Massive tuning forks vibrate constantly, warming the air. Sonic crystals glow with sound energy. It's beautiful and terrifying.

#### 10. The Scorched Wastes (Cult of the Eclipsed Flame)
**Location**: Volcanic badlands, lava fields  
**Light Source**: Lava glow, eternal fires  
**Heat Source**: Volcanic, geothermal, ritual pyres  
**Geography**: Obsidian fortresses, lava rivers  
**Population**: 15,000 fire cultists, flame-touched  
**Climate**: Scorching hot, bright with fire  

**Survival Strategy**: They worship the volcanic fury that replaced the sun. Living near active volcanoes, they have endless heat and light from lava. Fire magic amplifies it. They're the only faction that's actually THRIVING.

#### 11. The Null Zones (Children of the Hollow)
**Location**: Reality breaches, void-touched regions  
**Light Source**: Anti-light (void glow)  
**Heat Source**: They don't need heat - they're not fully real  
**Geography**: Non-Euclidean spaces, reality tears  
**Population**: Unknown - they exist between existence  
**Climate**: Timeless, spaceless, incomprehensible  

**Survival Strategy**: They've transcended physical needs by partially entering the void. They don't need light, heat, food, or air. They're becoming something else entirely. Terrifying but effective.

#### 12. The Surgical Academies (Ossuary Praxis)
**Location**: Victorian cities, medical districts  
**Light Source**: Chemical lights, bioluminescent experiments  
**Heat Source**: Biological furnaces, flesh-engines  
**Geography**: Gothic hospitals, experiment chambers  
**Population**: 18,000 surgeons, patients, experiments  
**Climate**: Warm (biological), sickly green light  

**Survival Strategy**: Mad science. They've created biological furnaces - living organs that generate heat. Bioluminescent creatures provide light. They've surgically adapted humans to need less warmth. Body horror, but they survive.

## Travel & Connections

**Natural Borders:**
- Frozen wastes separate north from south
- Mountain ranges divide east from west
- Rivers and chasms create barriers

**Faction Relationships:**
- **Neighbors** can trade, war, or ignore each other
- **Distant factions** rarely interact
- **Vertical factions** (sky/underground) have unique access

**Neutral Zones:**
- **Otter River Traders** - Connect water-adjacent factions
- **Badger Builder Clans** - Work in all territories
- **Fox Scholar Network** - Knowledge brokers everywhere

## Reputation System Design

### Base Reputation: 0 (Neutral)
- Can hire mercenaries from neutral factions
- Basic quests available
- No hero recruitment

### Friendly: 25+ 
- Faction heroes available for recruitment
- Better quest rewards
- Discount on services
- Welcome in faction territory

### Allied: 75+
- Faction leader joins you in RAID events
- Best quests unlocked
- Free services
- Honored guest status

### Unfriendly: -25 to -74
- Hostile dialogue
- No services
- Attacked if caught in territory

### Hostile: -75+
- Kill on sight
- Bounty on your head
- Cannot enter territory

### Logarithmic Reputation:
```
+50 with Dawnshield → +10 with other Light factions
+50 with Dawnshield → -25 with opposing Dark factions
+100 with Dawnshield → +25 with Light, -50 with Dark

The more committed you are, the more polarized you become.
OR stay neutral and hire mercenaries from everyone.
```

## This Changes EVERYTHING

We're not making 20 linear scenarios.

We're making a WORLD with 12 territories, each struggling to survive.

The player navigates this world, choosing allies, earning trust, building a warband.

The story emerges from the world, not imposed upon it.
